The 5 Countries That Spend the Most on Military Relative to National Income in 2023

Middle Eastern countries like Oman, Afghanistan, Lebanon, Kuwait, and Saudi Arabia are prioritizing high military expenditures due to their strategic positions and regional security dynamics. Oman spends 12% on defense due to its geographical location and instabilities, while Afghanistan allocates 10.6% of its national income to combat internal conflicts and terrorism. Lebanon spends 10.5% on defense to deal with regional security threats and neighboring countries. Kuwait allocates 7.1% to defend against regional security threats, while Saudi Arabia spends 7.1% due to its regional security role, energy dependence, and desire to intervene in power balances.

5 Countries with the Largest Economy in the world by Gross Domestic Product (GDP) in 2023

While the United States is the leader in the global economy with a share of 27%, China (17.7%) stands out with its high economic growth rates and industrial developments. Germany (4.43%) is an active actor in the global economy with a strong manufacturing sector, high quality products and export performance. While Japan (4.23%) stands out as an economic power with technological innovations and a strong industrial infrastructure, India's young and growing population (4.11%) draws attention with its growth potential in the service sector. The economic distribution between these countries plays an important role in shaping global trade.
